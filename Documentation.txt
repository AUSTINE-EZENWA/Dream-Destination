Home Page (PROMPT)
Hello, how are you doing. Please I am learning website development and I want you to Design a modern, visually immersive home page for a travel agency website called "Dream Destination Travel and Tours." The page should feel inspiring, trustworthy, and easy to navigate, encouraging visitors to start planning their next trip.
The home page should be built using the following features: 
•	Clean, contemporary travel-industry aesthetic with generous white space
•	Warm, wanderlust-inspiring color palette (e.g., deep ocean blue, sandy beige, sunset orange/coral as accent)
•	Large, high-quality full-bleed photography of destinations (beaches, mountains, cities, nature)
•	Smooth hover animations and subtle scroll transitions
•	Mobile-responsive layout
Sections to include:
1.	Navigation Bar — Logo (left), menu links (Home, Destinations, Tours, About, Blog, Contact), and a prominent "Book Now" or "Get a Quote" CTA button (right). Sticky on scroll.
2.	Hero Section — Full-width background video or slideshow of stunning destinations, overlaid with a bold headline (e.g., "Your Dream Destination Awaits"), a short supporting sub-headline, and a search bar/widget to find trips
3.	Why Choose Us — Icon-based section highlighting 3–4 value props (e.g., Best Price Guarantee, 24/7 Support, Expert Local Guides, Flexible Booking).
4.	Popular Tour Packages — Cards showing tour name, duration, price, rating, and a short description, with a "See All Packages" link.
5.	Testimonials — Carousel of customer reviews with photos, star ratings, and names.
6.	Newsletter/CTA Banner — Eye-catching strip inviting visitors to subscribe for travel deals, with an email input field and subscribe button.
7.	Footer — Logo, contact info, social media icons, quick links, payment/security badges, and copyright.
Tone: Adventurous, welcoming, and professional — making the visitor feel excited and confident about booking their next trip.

Booking Page
Create a fully responsive Booking Page for "Dream Destination Travel and Tours (DDTT)," a travel agency website. The page should include:
1.	Hero/Header section with the DDTT logo/brand name, a short tagline (e.g., "Book Your Dream Getaway Today"), and a travel-themed background image
2.	Multi-step booking form (or single scrollable form) with the following fields: 
o	Destination selection (dropdown or searchable list — e.g., Bali, Dubai, Paris, Maldives, etc.)
o	Trip type (Solo, Couple, Family, Group)
o	Check-in and Check-out / Travel dates (date pickers)
o	Number of Travelers (Adults/Children counter)
o	Package selection (Basic, Standard, Premium) with price display
o	Add-ons/extras (Airport pickup, Travel insurance, Tour guide, Hotel upgrade) as checkboxes
o	Full Name, Email, Phone Number
o	Special requests/notes (text area)
3.	Price summary sidebar that dynamically updates as the user selects package, add-ons, and number of travelers (showing subtotal, taxes/fees, and total price)
4.	Trust/assurance section with icons for "Secure Payment," "Best Price Guarantee," "24/7 Support," and "Free Cancellation"
5.	Payment method selection (Credit/Debit Card, PayPal, Bank Transfer) — UI only, no real transactions
6.	"Confirm Booking" CTA button with hover/loading animation
7.	Responsive design that adapts cleanly across mobile, tablet, and desktop (form stacks vertically on mobile, sidebar moves below form or collapses)
8.	Color scheme: ocean blue, sandy beige, and sunset orange to match the DDTT travel brand identity
9.	Include footer with social media handles and DDT contacts media
10.	Include a sticky/fixed price summary on desktop and a collapsible bottom bar summary on mobile

Destination Page
Create a fully responsive Destinations Page for "Dream Destination Travel and Tours (DDTT)," a travel agency website. The page should include:
1.	Hero section with a full-width travel/scenic background image, a heading like "Explore Our Top Destinations," and a short subheading
2.	Search & filter bar allowing users to filter destinations by: 
o	Region/Continent (Africa, Asia, Europe, Americas, etc.)
o	Trip type (Beach, Adventure, City, Honeymoon, Cultural, Wildlife)
o	Price range
o	Sort by (Popularity, Price Low-High, Price High-Low)
3.	Destination cards grid — each card should display: 
o	Destination image
o	Destination name & country
o	Short description (1–2 lines)
o	Starting price (e.g., "From $499")
o	Duration (e.g., "5 Days / 4 Nights")
o	Rating (stars or number)
o	"View Details" / "Book Now" button
o	Hover effect (image zoom or overlay with quick info)
4.	Featured/Popular destinations carousel at the top showcasing 4–6 highlighted locations
5.	Pagination or "Load More" button for browsing additional destinations
6.	Call-to-action banner (e.g., "Can't find your dream destination? Contact us for a custom trip!") linking to the contact/booking page
7.	Responsive grid layout: 
o	Desktop: 3–4 cards per row
o	Tablet: 2 cards per row
o	Mobile: 1 card per row, filters collapse into a dropdown/modal
8.	Color scheme: ocean blue, sandy beige, and sunset orange to match DDTT's travel brand identity
9.	Micro-interactions: smooth hover animations on cards, fade-in effects on scroll, animated filter transitions
10.	Ensure fast-loading optimized images and clean typography for readability across all devices
